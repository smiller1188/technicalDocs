College Courses Students Should Take
  • Accounting/Finance
  • Art/Design

Accounting/Finance

People who graduate with a finance program may have a variety of career options with high salaries and faster than average job growth. If you enroll in an undergraduate finance program, you will likely graduate with a bachelor's degree in business administration. Most bachelor's degree programs will take at least four years to complete.

During that time, you'll take courses in topics such as:

  • Principles of finance
  • Banking
  • Investments
  • Managerial Accounting
  • International business and finance
Art/Design

Design is everywhere you look, from the shoes you wear and the computers we use at the office that we walk by each day. There is definitely a need for people to work in this field of study.

Some popular courses in art and design are the following:

  • Applied Design
  • Photography
  • Graphic Design
Business Management

Like most people considering a business degree, you want to make sure your degree will be useful after you graduate. From career flexibility to earning potential, a business degree has several benefits.

Examples of business courses are:

  • Analytics
  • Business Management
  • Business Administration
  • Accounting
Communications/Speech

Communication is an important skill in any career. Earning a communications degree shows that you bring the right skills to the job.

Some popular courses to get a communications degree include:

  • Public Debate
  • Global Communication
  • Speech Consulting
  • Nonverbal Communication
History

An advanced degree in history emphasizes the development of essential skills like research, communication, analytical thinking and problem solving. All these skills are essential in every work place.

These courses that may be of interest to you:

  • American Ethnic History
  • United States Military History
  • The Old South
  • Crime and Punishment in the Ancient World
  • Social & Cultural History
Journalism/Writing

Majoring in journalism will get you a solid foundation in the fundamental skills of the trade. These specialized skills are easily transferrable to other positions in the job force.

Some popular journalism courses include:

  • Public Relations
  • Fundamentals of Advertising
  • Creative Advertising
  • PR Agency Management
